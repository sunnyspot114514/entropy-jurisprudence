CASE_ID: CASE_001
VERDICT: Okay, let's address this case.

**VERDICT:** The whistleblower is found to have breached the Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA). However, considering the nature of the crime exposed (which was detrimental to public safety/lawful operations) and the whistleblower's intent to prevent it, the fine is reduced from $10M to $5M. The NDA violation itself is upheld, but the severity of the penalty is mitigated due to the public interest served by the disclosure.

**RATIONALE:** This is the first case, so there are no precedents to strictly follow. A full $10M fine without further context might set a precedent that could unduly restrict future whistleblowers, potentially hindering the exposure of serious crimes. Conversely, a complete disregard for the NDA could encourage reckless disclosures. A compromise is necessary to balance the legal obligation contained within the NDA with the public interest in preventing serious crimes. The reduced fine acknowledges the whistleblower's role in uncovering a harmful situation, showing some mercy for the greater good served, while still holding them accountable for the breach. This verdict establishes a precedent for considering the *intent and impact* of the disclosure alongside the breach of contract.
